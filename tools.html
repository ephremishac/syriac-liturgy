The first public Syriac model on Transkribus

Model trained on the manuscript: ÖNB Cod. Syr. 1. Austrian National Library - Vienna (Österreichische Nationalbibliothek). The Syriac Gospels of Vienna scribed by Moses of Mardin in 1554 - Vienna. This first public Syriac model on Transkribus was prepared by the Syriac Group in IMAFO during the HTR Winter School 2024 - Vienna.

Try the model immediately without registration on the Transkribus website: The Syriac Gospels of Vienna (Serto).

See also the post in the Digital Orientalist: The first public Syriac HTR model on Transkribus.

A searchable website based on the dataset used for the training of the model is available: Vienna Syriac Gospel - Moses of Mardin, 1554.

See also the repositories on GitHub and on Zenodo.

The second public Syriac model on Transkribus

Model trained on the manuscript: MS 36, Saint Mark's Monastery, Jerusalem. The second public Syriac model on Transkribus was prepared by the Syriac Group in IMAFO during the HTR Winter School 2025, Vienna. With gratitude to the Syrian Orthodox Monastery of St. Mark, Jerusalem. The manuscript is made available courtesy of vHMML: SMMJ 00036.

Try the model "Syriac New Testament of Jerusalem (Estrangelo - Melkite)" even without registration on the Transkribus website.

See also the dataset and description on GitHub and on Zenodo and cataloged on HTR-United.
